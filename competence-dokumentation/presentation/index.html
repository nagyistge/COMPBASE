Introduction

  • Grades are relative (classmates, cohort, age) but are treated as static (i.e. for university entrance)
  • Grades were invented in a time of professions and disciplines but the shift to service sector blurred disciplines and professions
  • Grades are pseudo-objective: the numerical value implies a standardized evaluation of performance
  • Grades are an ordinal scale. However, student performance does not follow equidistant steps but is multidimensional and infinitely differentiable

Portfolio types

  • time dimension (continous, project based) [HPGH07b, S.39]
  • group or indvidual [BKM98]
  • pedagogical principles (learning process, competencies, company based) [Neu11]
  • scope (personal development portfolio, studies portfolio, school portfolio, course portfolio, internship portfolio) [Lam14, S.6]
  • goal (reflexion, assessment, presentation) [Lam14]

Competency portfolios

  • Competency portfolio is very useful for assessing future learning goals
  • Competency portfolio more objective then grades (formulated competencies are indicators for evaluation method)
  • Competencies are interchangeable between organizations without limiting the creativity of the teacher with centralized tests

Heutagogy and competence based assessment

  • Portfolios as a formative evaluation method offers potential to display individual learning processes
  • The competence portfolio model can be transferred to any stage of education

Formulating competencies


Structure:

  • Learner : operator : [action domain | knowledge domain]
    • List([(subcompetence)])

For instance:

  • Computer science students : implement : programs
    • (by programming : webservices)
    • (by designing : interfaces)

Natural language processing pipeline:

  • Dependency graph (extracting the operator, the subject and the knowledge tokens)
  • Stemming
  • Synonym database
  • Generic term database in order to determine subcompetences and supercompetences
